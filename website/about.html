Our History

SCIS, which started from a primary two-room nipa hut of light materials in 1915, was built to teach the people of Brgy. Sto. Cristo especially the young how to read and write. Values systems was also taught like respecting elders ang being an obedient person.
By 1930, the Gabaldon Type of school building was constructed on the site of 20,000 square meters (2 hectares) along Juan Luna Street of Brgy. Sto. Cristo, Tarlac, Tarlac. This was through the act. No. 1881 of the National Assembly called Gabaldon Law through the initiative of Isauro Gabaldon. It allocated school buildings nationwide from the budget of P10,000 by the National Government. Afterwards, the site was purchased by the Municipal Government and donated to the school on June 18, 1930.
The Gabaldon building has only (6) rooms with (2) offices for the principal and supervisor of the school. Another (2) rooms were added after a month. A small house was built for Home Economics (H.E.) and an Industrial Arts building in 1937.
Under the same period, a clinic was erected at the right side of the building for emergencies and first aid for the students. Health services like injection or vaccinations were held in the clinic. Two comfort rooms (one for the girls and another for the boys) were also constructed at the back of the storehouse beside clinic.

Mission

To protect and promote the right of every Filipino to quality, equitable, culture-based, and complete basic education where;

Student learn a in a child-friendly, gender-sensitive, safe, and motivating environment
Teachers facilitate a learning and constantly nurture every learner.
Administrators and staffs, as stewards of the institution, ensure on enabling and supportive environment for effective learning to happen.
Family, community, and other stakeholders are actively engaged and share responsibility for developing life-long learners.

Vision

We dream of Filipinos who passionately love their country and whose values and competencies enable them to realize their full potential and contribute meaningfully to building the nation.

As a learner-centered public institution, the Department of Education continuously improves itself to better serve its stakeholders.
